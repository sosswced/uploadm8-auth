UploadM8 respects intellectual property rights and complies with the Digital Millennium Copyright Act (DMCA), 17 U.S.C. § 512. We process valid takedown notices promptly and maintain a repeat infringer policy as required by law to preserve our safe harbor protections.
1

Platform Scope & Safe Harbor

UploadM8 operates as a video upload and distribution pipeline. We facilitate the publishing of content to third-party platforms (TikTok, YouTube, Instagram, Facebook) at the direction of our users. We qualify as a service provider under DMCA Section 512(c) and maintain safe harbor protections by:

  • Not having actual knowledge of infringing content stored on our systems
  • Expeditiously removing or disabling access to content upon receiving valid notices
  • Maintaining and enforcing a repeat infringer termination policy
  • Designating a DMCA agent with the U.S. Copyright Office
Important scope note: UploadM8 controls the upload pipeline and our storage infrastructure. We do not control content once it is published to TikTok, YouTube, Instagram, or Facebook. Takedown requests for content already live on those platforms must be directed to each respective platform using their own DMCA/copyright reporting tools.
2

DMCA Takedown Notice Requirements

To file a valid DMCA takedown notice, your written communication to dmca@uploadm8.com must include all of the following:

  • A physical or electronic signature of the copyright owner or a person authorized to act on their behalf
  • Identification of the copyrighted work claimed to be infringed (or a representative list if multiple works are covered by a single notice)
  • Identification of the allegedly infringing material and information sufficient for us to locate it on our systems (e.g., the specific URL, upload date, or UploadM8 job ID)
  • Your contact information: name, mailing address, telephone number, and email address
  • A statement that you have a good faith belief that the use of the material is not authorized by the copyright owner, its agent, or the law
  • A statement, made under penalty of perjury, that the information in the notification is accurate and that you are the copyright owner or authorized to act on the copyright owner's behalf
Intentionally submitting a materially false DMCA notice exposes you to liability under DMCA Section 512(f) for damages, costs, and attorneys' fees. Submit notices only for content you have a good faith belief infringes your copyright.

Incomplete notices will be rejected without action. We will contact you via the email address provided to request any missing information.

3

Our DMCA Process

Upon receiving a complete, valid DMCA notice:

  1. We will acknowledge receipt within 2 business days
  2. We will review the notice for completeness and apparent validity
  3. If valid, we will expeditiously remove or disable access to the identified content in our systems
  4. We will notify the user who uploaded the content that their material has been removed and provide a copy of the notice (with your contact information)
  5. We will provide the user with information about filing a counter-notice
  6. If the content was already published to social platforms, we will advise the user to remove it from those platforms directly and will advise the claimant to use platform-specific reporting tools

For valid, actionable notices, we target content removal within 10 business days of receipt of a complete notice.

4

Counter-Notice Procedure

If you believe your content was removed in error (e.g., due to misidentification or a claim of fair use), you may submit a counter-notice to dmca@uploadm8.com. Your counter-notice must include:

  • Your physical or electronic signature
  • Identification of the removed material and its location before removal
  • A statement, under penalty of perjury, that you have a good faith belief the material was removed due to mistake or misidentification
  • Your name, address, phone number, and email address
  • A statement consenting to jurisdiction of the Federal District Court for your district (or Jackson County, Missouri if outside the U.S.)

Upon receiving a valid counter-notice, we will forward it to the original claimant. If the claimant does not notify us of a court action within 10–14 business days, we may restore the removed material at our discretion.

5

Repeat Infringer Policy

UploadM8 maintains a repeat infringer policy as required by DMCA Section 512(i):

  • First valid DMCA strike: Formal warning issued. Content removed. User notified of our repeat infringer policy.
  • Second valid DMCA strike: Publishing privileges suspended for 30 days. Account flagged for review.
  • Third valid DMCA strike: Account terminated permanently. No refunds issued for any remaining subscription period.

Strikes may be contested through the counter-notice process. Successful counter-notices will not count toward the strike total. We reserve the right to terminate accounts after fewer strikes if the infringement is egregious (e.g., systematic copyright theft or commercial piracy).
