AIC Tokens & Upload credits (PUT)

AI Content (AIC) tokens are consumed when using AI-powered features in the upload pipeline (captions, titles, thumbnail briefs, vision stages, and similar). Upload / processing credits (PUT) meter general processing capacity. Some tools (for example Thumbnail Studio and Pikzels-backed routes) debit PUT and/or AIC per our pricing tables when you run them. Tokens and credits are non-refundable and non-transferable unless required by law. Unused balances may not roll over between billing periods unless your plan explicitly says otherwise. Top-up packs may be purchased at current pricing.

AI Processing — pipeline & third parties

When AI features are enabled, your video may be processed by stages including:

  • Frame extraction & language/vision models: keyframes and prompts are sent to OpenAI (e.g. GPT-4o or GPT-4o-mini, configurable) to generate captions, titles, and hashtags. This step typically consumes AIC tokens.
  • Audio transcription (Whisper): if your video contains a speech/audio stream, a temporary audio extract is sent to OpenAI Whisper. This step is billed at the infrastructure level and does not consume AIC tokens. You may disable related options in Settings → Preferences (speech-to-text / audio context).
  • Optional vendors: depending on your plan and our deployment configuration, stages may also call Google Cloud (Vision / Video Intelligence), Twelve Labs, ACRCloud, Replicate, fal.ai, remove.bg, or Pikzels for thumbnails and analysis. Those subprocessors process data under their own terms — see Subprocessors.

We do not use your content to train UploadM8-owned models. Third-party retention and training rules are governed by each vendor’s terms; see Subprocessors and our Privacy Policy.
