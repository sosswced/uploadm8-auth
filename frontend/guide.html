Quick Start — First Upload
From sign-up to live in under 5 minutes
Your first video goes everywhere at once
UploadM8 lets you upload a single video file and automatically publish it to TikTok, YouTube Shorts, Instagram Reels, and Facebook Reels simultaneously — no manual re-uploading.
  1. Connect accounts — Go to Connected Accounts and authorize each platform via OAuth. Takes about 60 seconds per platform.
  2. Upload a video — Head to Upload, drag your file in, choose your platforms, add a caption, and hit Upload.
  3. Watch it process — UploadM8 transcodes, validates, and publishes. Check the Queue for live status.
  4. Check results — Once done, each platform chip in the Dashboard shows a direct link to your live post.
Pro tip: Your first upload uses PUT tokens from your wallet. Free includes 120 PUT / 1,200 AIC per month (released daily, UTC); paid tiers scale up to 22,000 PUT / 140,000 AIC per month. Unused credits roll over. Each upload costs 10+ PUT depending on platforms and features.
PUT & AIC Wallet
Understanding your upload credits and AI credits
PUT Tokens — Upload Processing Credits
Each upload job costs PUT tokens. They cover the processing, transcoding, and publishing costs. Your plan allowance is released daily (UTC); unused balance rolls over month to month.

PUT cost per job is deterministic — you always know exactly what you'll spend before uploading:

ItemPUT cost
Base (any upload)10 PUT
Each additional publish target beyond the 1st (platform or account)+2 PUT
HUD burn-in overlay+5 PUT
Priority/turbo processing lane+5 PUT
Each additional thumbnail beyond 1st+1 PUT
Example: Uploading to 3 publish targets (e.g. 3 platforms or 3 selected accounts) with HUD = 10 + (2×2) + 5 = 19 PUT
Important: PUT is reserved when you submit an upload, then debited when the job succeeds. If it fails, the hold is released automatically — you don't lose PUT on failures.

Where your settings actually live (and what wins)
UploadM8 merges several stores before a job runs. Knowing this explains “I toggled it but nothing changed” and duplicate-looking fields.

The worker builds one effective preference object per upload. Rough precedence:

  1. user_preferences (Save All Preferences) — Auto captions/thumbnails, styled thumbs, thumbnail interval, default privacy, hashtag lists, Trill toggles (Enable Drive Analysis, min score, AI enhancement, Trill model), audio master + per-service AI flags, email/discord fields, and more. This is the main “upload defaults” table.
  2. users.preferences (caption / hashtag overlay) — Caption style, tone, voice, AI caption scan depth slider, and some hashtag/caption fields loaded via profile APIs can overlay here. When both exist, this layer can override specific keys for merged results.
  3. user_settings — Speed HUD burn-in is read from hud_enabled here (plus HUD position, speed thresholds, colors, etc.). The Trill HUD checkbox in Settings is saved so presign cost and the HUD stage see the same flag.
Practical rule: After changing anything material, use Save All Preferences on Settings. If you only moved sliders without saving, the next upload still uses the last persisted snapshot — the queue never guesses.
Scheduled & queued jobs: Jobs typically snapshot preferences around submit time. Changing settings mid-queue affects new submissions first; already-queued work may have been priced and configured with the older snapshot.

Start here: preflight checklist before every upload
Most issues are a missing dependency (speech-to-text needs audio context), a plan limit, or Use AI turned off on the upload form. Work through this once; then behavior is predictable.
  1. Plan + wallet — Match tier to intent: Free includes AI but with watermarks, tight frame caps, and no HUD. Confirm PUT and AIC in Billing; presign reserves a hold against both when AI/HUD/thumbnails are in play.
  2. Persist everything — In Settings → Preferences, set toggles and click Save All Preferences. That path persists preferences, syncs HUD where applicable, and saves platform colors in one action. Browser-only changes never reach the worker.
  3. Upload form: Use AI — On Upload, Use AI must be on for GPT captions, hashtag AI, and AI thumbnail selection/styling for that batch. Turning it off is intentional “dumb publish”: transcoding and publish can still run, but AI stages are skipped for cost and predictability.
  4. Telemetry pairs — Drive Insights, Trill scoring, and speed HUD need a .map alongside the video in the same submission (matching basename or whatever your export tool produces). Mark telemetry when your uploader asks — mismatched or missing maps mean no score and no map-based HUD.
  5. Dependency chains — Order matters: Enable Audio Context before expecting transcription or audio summaries. Auto-generate Thumbnails before Styled Thumbnails. Enable Drive Analysis before Telemetry Insights matters for captions. See the dependency tables below.
  6. Best-effort pipeline — Even with every toggle on, a stage can skip (vendor timeout, missing API key on the deployment, clip length limits). The job can still succeed; open Queue → job detail to see which stages ran vs skipped.
Two different “personas”: Caption Voice / Persona (Caption & AI) shapes how GPT writes titles and captions. Thumbnail Studio persona (persona UUID + strength) applies only to optional Aurora/Pikzels-style renders — they never substitute for each other.
Ready-made setup profiles (copy one, then tune)
Use these as baselines — every profile still requires Save All Preferences and appropriate plan tier.
Minimum cost / fast queue

Turn off Scene Understanding, Video Analyzer, Music Detection, and Emotion Signals unless you need them. Keep Caption Writer on only if you want AI copy. Lower AI Caption Scan Depth to the minimum that still reads well for your format. Disable AI-Generated Hashtags if you hand-pick tags.

Balanced creator (default quality)

Auto captions + Auto thumbnails on, Styled thumbnails on if your tier allows. Frame Inspector + Speech-to-Text on. Audio Context on with selective audio sub-services. Scan depth around mid-tier for your plan.

Maximum context (long-form, story-heavy)

Raise AI Caption Scan Depth toward your plan cap. Enable Video Analyzer / Scene Understanding only on deployments where those integrations are configured (otherwise they no-op). Keep transcription on for spoken narrative. Expect higher AIC holds.

Drive / motorsports / speed content

Enable Drive Analysis + upload .map. For Trill language in captions, add Telemetry Insights + AI Content Enhancement (subject to min score). For overlay only, enable Speed HUD and tune MPH thresholds; you can leave Telemetry Insights off if you do not want map data in the caption model.

Talk-first / podcast clips

Enable Audio Context, Speech-to-Text, and Audio Content Summary on; you can lower scan depth if the visual is static. Style captions toward factual or story with a calm or authentic tone.

Platform Colors — badges on styled thumbnails
Per-platform hex colors apply to styled thumbnail templates (badge and focal accents). Accent color can influence highlight strokes.
  1. In Settings → Preferences, scroll to Platform Colors.
  2. Set TikTok, YouTube, Instagram, and Facebook colors — these map to each platform’s styled output.
  3. Set Accent if you want a consistent highlight color for focal cues (where the template uses it).
  4. Click Save All Preferences so colors are stored with your account.
Requires styled thumbnails: Colors affect the composite/template path. If Auto Thumbnails or styled composites are off — or your plan is template-only — you may not see custom badge colors until those features are active.
AI Caption Scan Depth — frames per video
Higher numbers give the model more visual context (and use more AIC). Your plan caps the maximum; the UI shows the allowed range.
  1. Turn on Auto-generate Captions and keep Caption and Hashtag Writer on in AI Service Controls.
  2. Set AI Caption Scan Depth in Upload Preferences to match your content — fast cuts may need more frames; simple talking-head clips may need fewer.
  3. Stay at or below your plan’s frame cap (see Plan limits — up to 15 frames on Studio/Agency; lower tiers cap earlier).
  4. Save All Preferences, then upload — scan depth applies to jobs that start processing after the save completes.
Choosing a number: For jump-cut vlogs or multi-location days, bias higher (within your cap) so the model sees distinct beats. For single-angle rants or screen recordings, 3–5 frames often suffice and trims AIC.
Caption & AI Settings — global writing style
Applies when the pipeline generates captions. Manual title/caption text you enter on the upload form is treated as authoritative — AI does not overwrite your locked-in copy unless your workflow explicitly replaces it downstream.

Three independent axes are combined in the prompt. Think of them as structure, mood, and character:

punchyHook-forward, short clauses, emphasis on the first line. Strong for Shorts/Reels retention.
factualNeutral, descriptive lead; minimizes flourish. Good for tutorials, newsy clips, product demos.
Caption ToneWhat it does

calmSteady, conversational, avoids aggressive CTAs.

defaultBalanced creator voice — no strong caricature.

  1. Pick style, tone, and voice to match your channel promise — misalignment (e.g. factual + hypebeast) can work ironically but often reads inconsistent.
  2. If Trill AI model is exposed separately for drive-enhanced captions, that selector only affects the telemetry-enriched caption path; general caption model picks may live in the same settings card.
  3. Save All Preferences; changes apply to the next processed jobs, not retroactively to published posts.
Together: These dimensions plus scan depth, transcript, frame inspector labels, and optional map/telemetry context all feed the same caption stage — starving any upstream feature (e.g. no transcript when you talk for 60s) limits what GPT can say no matter how strong the persona is.
Hashtag Settings — always-on, per platform, AI, caps
Hashtags are merged from several sources, deduped, filtered, then clamped. Understanding order prevents surprises on publish.
  1. Turn on AI-Generated Hashtags when you want the model to propose tags (consumes AIC alongside caption generation when Use AI is on).
  2. Set AI hashtag count (how many AI tags to aim for) and style — e.g. trending vs niche vs mixed — to bias discovery vs community-specific tags.
  3. Add Always Include tags for brand or campaign tags you need on every post regardless of platform.
  4. Fill Platform-specific lists (TikTok, YouTube, Instagram, Facebook) when the same caption should carry different tag mixes per destination.
  5. Maintain Blocked for banned, off-brand, or legally sensitive tags — blocked wins against every other source, including AI.
  6. Set Max hashtags and Hashtag position (start, end, or caption embedding where supported) to match each platform’s best practice.
Merge intuition: At presign, manual upload tags are normalized and deblocked first. Always-include and platform lists merge in downstream publish logic; AI tags append when enabled. Duplicates collapse; blocked tags never appear. If your list exceeds max hashtags, the tail is cut — raise the cap modestly or shorten always-on lists.
Compliance: AI suggestions are probabilistic. Keep blocked lists updated for regulated niches (finance, health, contests) and audit a few publishes after big setting changes.
Drive Insights (BETA) — telemetry, Trill score, captions, HUD
Four related concepts: (1) ingesting the .map, (2) scoring the run, (3) feeding captions, (4) burning a speed HUD. They share files but use different toggles.

Audio Intelligence
Master switch first; then fine-tune sub-services. Audio off is a hard stop for every dependent stage.
  1. Turn on Enable Audio Context (use_audio_context). Off → transcription, music ID, environmental audio classifiers, summaries, and emotion-from-audio paths do not run.
  2. Enable Speech-to-text in Upload Preferences for Whisper-style transcripts, and keep Speech-to-Text enabled under AI Service Controls — both ends must agree.
  3. Use AI Service Controls to disable expensive detectors individually: music fingerprinting, audio event labels, semantic audio summary, emotion signals — each saves some AIC when you don’t need that modality.
  4. Expect graceful skips when the source clip has no usable audio track, is extremely short, or blows duration limits — the pipeline continues without transcript context.
Why summaries matter: Even if you disable vision-heavy features, a short Audio Content Summary often helps GPT understand “what the viewer hears” for music-forward or ambient storytelling clips.

Notifications, email, Discord, scheduled alerts
These settings do not change AI quality, but they change how quickly you notice failures, quota issues, or finished jobs.
  • Email notifications — Master email on/off for product mail (transactional + optional marketing depending on your account flags).
  • Auth security alerts — Login anomalies and password events; keep on for managed accounts.
  • Digest emails — Periodic summaries so you don’t live inside the Queue UI.
  • Scheduled alert emails — Pings when scheduled posts fail or need attention.
  • Discord webhook — Paste a channel webhook URL to mirror key job events into Discord for teams.
Webhook URLs are secrets — treat them like API keys. Rotate if leaked.

Upload System
The full pipeline from file → live post
Video processing pipeline
Your video goes through a multi-stage pipeline: upload to R2 storage → FFmpeg transcode per platform spec → AI analysis → publish to each platform.

Typical processing order (skips any stage you turned off or that isn’t needed):

  1. Presign — Frontend requests a secure upload URL. Wallet is checked; PUT/AIC is reserved as a hold.
  2. R2 Upload — File goes directly to Cloudflare R2 (not through the app server).
  3. Worker download — Worker pulls the file to temp storage.
  4. Telemetry / map — If a .map file is present and settings allow, route and Trill data are parsed (needed for Drive captions and/or HUD).
  5. HUD burn-in — If enabled and your plan supports it, speed overlay is composited on the working video.
  6. Watermark — Applied when your plan uses watermarks.
  7. Transcode — FFmpeg produces per-platform video variants.
  8. Audio & vision AI — Audio context, optional scene / video intelligence, frame inspection (per your AI Service toggles).
  9. Thumbnails — Frames picked and styled per settings.
  10. Captions — Titles, captions, hashtags generated when enabled.
  11. Publish — Outputs go to each selected account via platform APIs.
  12. Settle wallet — On success the hold is captured; on failure it is released.
Best-effort: A stage may skip (timeout, missing vendor key, clip limits) while the rest of the pipeline continues — the job can still succeed with partial AI.
File requirements: MP4, MOV, AVI, MKV or WebM. Max 4GB. Recommended: 1080×1920 (9:16 portrait) for short-form platforms.

Audio Transcription (Whisper)
All Plans NEW
Speech-aware AI captions via OpenAI Whisper
Before generating captions, UploadM8 now extracts the audio from your video, sends it to OpenAI Whisper, and injects the transcript into every AI prompt — so GPT-4o knows exactly what was said, not just what was shown.

Pipeline position (Stage 5.5):

  1. Transcode completes — video is in its final format, audio codec confirmed.
  2. FFmpeg extracts audio — a temporary WAV file is created from the video's audio stream.
  3. Whisper transcribes — the WAV is sent to OpenAI Whisper (whisper-1). Transcript is capped at 3,000 characters.
  4. Transcript is injected — caption_stage receives ctx.ai_transcript alongside the video frames, producing grounded, accurate captions.
  5. Temp file is deleted — the WAV is cleaned up with the job's temp directory.
Example: A 60s driving clip where the narrator says "Coming into the final chicane here at Laguna Seca" will generate captions mentioning Laguna Seca — not just a generic driving description.
