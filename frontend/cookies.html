1

Cookies & browser storage

Cookies are small text files sites can store in your browser. sessionStorage and localStorage are separate browser APIs that work like on-device key/value stores.

How UploadM8 signs you in today: the web app stores JWT access and refresh tokens in sessionStorage (primary). Legacy tokens in localStorage are migrated once and then removed. We do not rely on HttpOnly session cookies for API authentication in the current SPA.

Third-party page assets: some pages load styles or scripts from CDNs (for example Font Awesome or Google Fonts). Those providers may set or read their own cookies under their policies.

2

What we store in your browser

Required for the app
Core

Access and refresh tokens, cached user snapshot, API base override, and similar keys in sessionStorage (and one-time migration from localStorage). Clearing site data or using private browsing will sign you out or reset state.

Examples: uploadm8_access_token, uploadm8_refresh_token, uploadm8_cached_user
Duration: Until logout or tab/session clear (see auth code in frontend/js/auth-stack.js)
Party: First-party (your browser only)
Preferences & UI
Comfort

Theme (uploadm8_theme), dashboard card order, upload-session helpers, and similar values in sessionStorage/localStorage. They improve UX but are not strictly required to reach the login screen.

Purpose: Dark/light mode, layout, in-progress upload hints
Duration: Session or until you clear site data
Party: First-party
Errors & reliability
If configured

On the server, we may send redacted error and performance events to Sentry when enabled — that is not a browser cookie, but it is part of our overall telemetry story. See Subprocessors → Sentry.

Browser footprint: No dedicated UploadM8 analytics cookie today
Advertising: None

Managing storage & third parties

Browser controls

Use your browser’s “clear site data” / “cookies and site data” tools for app.uploadm8.com (or the hostname you use). That will sign you out and remove saved layout/theme keys.

Blocking third-party cookies

Strict blocking may interfere with CDN-loaded fonts or icons. If the UI looks wrong, allow first-party scripts and styles for UploadM8 or self-host assets (enterprise setups).

Do Not Track

We do not respond to DNT signals in a standardized way today; we already avoid behavioral ad networks.
