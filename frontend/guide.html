Quick Start — First Upload
From sign-up to live in under 5 minutes
Your first video goes everywhere at once
UploadM8 lets you upload a single video file and automatically publish it to TikTok, YouTube Shorts, Instagram Reels, and Facebook Reels simultaneously — no manual re-uploading.
  1. Connect accounts — Go to Connected Accounts and authorize each platform via OAuth. Takes about 60 seconds per platform.
  2. Upload a video — Head to Upload, drag your file in, choose your platforms, add a caption, and hit Upload.
  3. Watch it process — UploadM8 transcodes, validates, and publishes. Check the Queue for live status.
  4. Check results — Once done, each platform chip in the Dashboard shows a direct link to your live post.
Pro tip: Your first upload uses PUT tokens from your wallet. Free plans get 60 PUT/month; paid plans get 300–4500. Each upload costs 10+ PUT depending on platforms and features.
PUT & AIC Wallet
Understanding your upload credits and AI credits
⚡
PUT Tokens — Upload Processing Credits
Each upload job costs PUT tokens. They cover the processing, transcoding, and publishing costs. Your wallet refills automatically every billing period.

PUT cost per job is deterministic — you always know exactly what you'll spend before uploading:

ItemPUT cost
Base (any upload)10 PUT
Each additional publish target beyond the 1st (platform or account)+2 PUT
HUD burn-in overlay+5 PUT
Priority/turbo processing lane+5 PUT
Each additional thumbnail beyond 1st+1 PUT
Example: Uploading to 3 publish targets (e.g. 3 platforms or 3 selected accounts) with HUD = 10 + (2×2) + 5 = 19 PUT
Important: PUT is reserved when you submit an upload, then debited when the job succeeds. If it fails, the hold is released automatically — you don't lose PUT on failures.

  2. Click Connect next to the platform you want
  3. You'll be redirected to the platform's login page — sign in and approve access

Upload System
The full pipeline from file → live post
Video processing pipeline
Your video goes through a multi-stage pipeline: upload to R2 storage → FFmpeg transcode per platform spec → AI analysis → publish to each platform.

Pipeline stages in order:

  1. Presign — Frontend requests a secure upload URL. Wallet is checked, PUT/AIC is reserved as a hold.
  2. R2 Upload — File goes directly to Cloudflare R2 (never touches the API server).
  3. Download to worker — Worker pulls the file from R2 to local temp storage.
  4. FFmpeg transcode — Video is re-encoded to each platform's required spec (resolution, codec, bitrate, aspect ratio).
  5. AI analysis — If enabled, AI scans keyframes for captions, hashtags, thumbnails.
  6. HUD burn-in — If enabled, overlay is composited onto the video.
  7. Publish — Transcoded versions are uploaded to each connected account via platform APIs.
  8. Settle wallet — On success: hold is captured (PUT/AIC debited). On failure: hold is released (no cost).
File requirements: MP4, MOV, AVI, MKV or WebM. Max 4GB. Recommended: 1080×1920 (9:16 portrait) for short-form platforms.

Scheduler
Creator Lite+
Schedule uploads in advance
Pick an exact date and time for your upload to go live, or use Smart Scheduling to let UploadM8 choose peak engagement times automatically.

How it works:

  1. In the upload form, toggle Schedule on instead of "Upload now"
  2. Choose Fixed time (specific date/time) or Smart (AI picks the optimal slot)
  3. Submit — the job enters a staged state and sits in the queue
  4. Worker pre-processes the video ahead of the scheduled time (lookahead window)
  5. At your chosen time, the publish stage fires automatically

Lookahead explained: A 24-hour lookahead means your video is fully transcoded and ready 24 hours before publish time. This prevents delays at the exact publish moment.
Job Queue
Track every upload from pending to published
Upload statuses explained
Every upload moves through a predictable state machine. Knowing each status helps you understand what's happening at any point.
StatusMeaning
● stagedFile uploaded, waiting to be processed (scheduled jobs sit here until lookahead window)
● queuedJob is in the Redis queue, waiting for a worker slot
● processingWorker is actively transcoding, running AI, compositing HUD
● ready_to_publishAll processing done, waiting for scheduled publish time
● publishingActively sending to platform APIs
● succeededPublished to all requested platforms successfully
● partialPublished to some platforms but at least one failed
● failedCould not publish. PUT hold is automatically released.
● cancelledYou cancelled before publishing. PUT hold released.
Account Groups
Organize accounts into named groups for bulk publishing
Group multiple accounts for one-click publishing
Create groups like "Fitness Brand", "Client A", or "All TikToks" — then select the group in the upload form instead of picking accounts one by one.
  1. Go to Account Groups and click New Group
  2. Name your group and add any connected accounts to it
  3. In the upload form, choose your group from the platform selector
  4. UploadM8 publishes to every account in the group simultaneously
Agency use case: Create one group per client brand. A single upload to the "Client A" group posts to their TikTok, Instagram, YouTube, and Facebook accounts all at once.
AI Engine
Creator Lite+
AI caption, hashtag & description generation
UploadM8's AI samples keyframes from your video and generates platform-optimized captions, trending hashtags, and descriptions using GPT-4o vision.

How AI analysis works:

  1. FFmpeg extracts keyframes at 5%, 50%, and 95% of your video (anchors — always taken)
  2. Additional frames are sampled between anchors up to your plan's max_caption_frames limit
  3. Frames + your video title are sent to GPT-4o with platform-specific prompts
  4. AI returns captions, hashtag suggestions, and optional descriptions
  5. You can edit the AI output before publishing, or auto-accept it

HUD Overlay
Creator Pro+
Burn a custom overlay into your video
The HUD (Heads-Up Display) lets you composite a branded overlay — logo, username, CTA text, progress bar — directly into your video's pixels before publishing.
  1. Configure your HUD template in Settings → Video Preferences
  2. Toggle "HUD Overlay" on in the upload form
  3. The worker composites your overlay using FFmpeg before publishing
  4. The burned-in version is what gets published to every platform
Why burn-in matters: Platform overlays (like TikTok's UI) can cover your branding. By burning it in before upload, your branding is guaranteed visible at the pixel level regardless of platform chrome.
Cost: HUD burn adds +5 PUT per upload. Toggle it per upload — you don't have to use it every time.

Webhooks
Creator Lite+
Get notified when uploads complete
Configure a webhook URL in Settings and UploadM8 will POST a JSON payload to your endpoint every time an upload succeeds, partially succeeds, or fails.

Example webhook payload:

{"event":"upload.completed","upload_id":"abc123","status":"succeeded","platforms":["tiktok","youtube"],"put_cost":14,"timestamp":"2025-01-01T12:00:00Z"}
Use cases: Trigger a Zapier workflow, update a Notion database, post to Slack, or update your own CMS when content goes live.

Priority Queue System
Creator Pro+
Your uploads jump the queue
Paid tiers on Pro and above are routed to dedicated priority Redis queues. Their jobs are always processed before any free-tier uploads in the system.
Priority ClassQueuePlans
p0 — Highestprocess:priorityAgency, Lifetime, Admin
p1process:priorityStudio
p2process:priorityCreator Pro
p3process:normalCreator Lite
p4 — Lowestprocess:normalFree
BRPOP order: Workers always check process:priority first. The priority queue fully drains before a single normal-queue job is processed. An Agency upload literally jumps every Free-tier upload in the system.
Cost: Being on a priority plan adds +5 PUT to your upload cost. This covers the resource premium for guaranteed fast processing.
